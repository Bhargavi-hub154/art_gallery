Our Artists

Josine Vignon (1922–2022)

French artist known for mid-century pastel paintings of hillside towns and sunlit villages.

Edward Seago (1910–1974)

British painter admired for serene landscapes and coastal towns painted in muted tones.

Leonid Afremov (1955–2019)

Belarusian-Israeli impressionist famous for dreamy, softly colored cityscapes.

John Atkinson Grimshaw (1836–1893)

Victorian painter celebrated for atmospheric moonlit city scenes.

Jean Béraud (1849–1935)

French realist capturing elegant Parisian life with soft lighting and vintage charm.

Camille Pissarro (1830–1903)

Impressionist master of rural and city landscapes, often painting in delicate pastel tones.

Isaac Levitan (1860–1900)

Russian landscape painter known for poetic and minimalist scenes of nature.

Elaina Arty (Contemporary)

Contemporary painter working with pastel cityscapes and still lifes in soft blues, mints, and peach.

Pierre Bonnard (1867–1947)

Post-Impressionist who used warm, harmonious colors to capture intimate interiors and nature scenes.

Albert Bierstadt (1830–1902)

German-American painter known for grand landscapes of the American West.

Henri Fantin-Latour (1836–1904)

French painter of soft, realistic floral still lifes—elegant compositions with subtle color and delicate detail.
